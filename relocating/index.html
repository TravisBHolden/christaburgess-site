Common Questions for International Buyers

What is the CIPS designation and why does it matter?

The CIPS (Certified International Property Specialist) designation is awarded by the National Association of REALTORS and held by fewer than 4,000 agents worldwide. It signifies training in cross-border tax implications (FIRPTA, tax treaties), ITIN lending, international wire transfer protocols, and the cultural and logistical complexities of moving a family across continents.

Christa Burgess holds the CIPS designation and has guided families relocating from Norway, the UK, Scotland, India, Mexico, Canada, and beyond. Working with a CIPS-certified agent means your agent has studied the specific complexities of your move.

Which Katy neighborhoods are most popular with international families?

Cinco Ranch, Cross Creek Ranch, and Grand Lakes are the three communities most commonly chosen by international families. Cinco Ranch has one of the highest concentrations of international residents in the region — its established South Asian, East Asian, European, and Latin American communities make cultural adjustment faster.

All three neighborhoods feed primarily into Katy ISD, one of the top school districts in Texas, with well-developed ESL programs and international student integration.

Can international families get a mortgage in Texas?

Yes. Foreign national loan programs are available and typically require 25–40% down payment with alternative income documentation. ITIN loans are available for buyers with a US Individual Taxpayer Identification Number. Many international buyers — especially those on corporate assignments — purchase with cash or use employer relocation packages.

How long does it take to find and close on a home when relocating internationally?

Begin the conversation at least 90 days before your target move date. The home search typically takes 2–4 weeks; the Texas contract-to-close timeline is 30–45 days. Most international buyers working with Christa are in a new home within 60–90 days of their first consultation.
