Katy vs. Fulshear

The Essential Question

Two Great Communities. Different Lives.

Most relocating families narrow their search to this single question within the first week of looking at west Houston. Both communities offer top-rated schools, resort-style amenities, and the kind of master-planned infrastructure that draws families from across the country. But they are not interchangeable. The differences are real, and they compound over time.

Here is the honest side-by-side.

Side by Side

The Comparison

Schools & Districts

Katy

Katy ISD — one of the top-rated districts in Texas and nationally recognized. 90,000+ students, ~80 campuses. Cinco Ranch HS and Seven Lakes HS are the flagship high schools. Consistent AP program performance and college placement rates.

Fulshear

Lamar CISD serves most of Fulshear, including Cross Creek Ranch and Weston Lakes. Fulshear HS is a newer, well-regarded campus. Some Cross Creek Ranch sections fall in Katy ISD — always verify the specific address before going under contract.

Home Prices & Lot Sizes

Katy

Wide range: starter homes in the $300s to luxury estates above $1M in premium Cinco Ranch sections. Lots tend to be smaller on the Houston-facing side of the community — established neighborhoods were built to earlier density standards. More resale inventory available.

Fulshear

Generally newer construction. Lots tend to be larger for the same price point — 65–70 ft lots are common where Katy might offer 50 ft. Entry-level starts around $350K in Cross Creek Ranch. Estate sections run $800K–$1.5M+. New construction inventory still available.

Commute to Energy Corridor

Katy (Cinco Ranch)

15–25 minutes off-peak via I-10. Peak hour: 30–45 min, managed lanes available. Downtown Houston: 30–45 min off-peak. The managed toll lanes on I-10 are a genuine game-changer for Katy commuters.

Fulshear (Cross Creek Ranch)

25–40 minutes off-peak — add 10–15 min to any Katy estimate. FM 1093 to TX-99 to I-10 is the primary route. The distance is real; budget honestly for daily commuters. Remote workers and occasional commuters fare much better here.

Community Character

Katy

More established, more infrastructure. La Centerra, Memorial Hermann Katy, major retail along I-10. Denser in some sections. The kind of community where everything is already built — schools, hospitals, restaurants. Higher walkability to commercial centers in select neighborhoods.

Fulshear

Frontier energy — the FM 1093 corridor is actively building out. New restaurants, a growing H-E-B, boutiques arriving regularly. Feels newer because it is newer. Less traffic density than Katy's busier corridors. A community still writing its own story, which many buyers find energizing.

Age of Development

Katy

Cinco Ranch began development in the late 1980s. Mature trees, established schools, proven HOA governance. The trade-off: fewer new construction options; buyers primarily working from resale inventory with occasional builder sections still completing.

Fulshear

Cross Creek Ranch launched in the mid-2000s and is still actively building out. New construction readily available across a range of builders and price points. Newer homes mean newer systems, warranties, and the ability to select finishes. The tree canopy is still maturing.

Honest Assessment

Christa's Read

✦ Who Belongs in Katy

You're commuting to the Energy Corridor or downtown Houston five days a week. You want your kids in Katy ISD specifically — and you're close enough to the school boundaries that it matters which section you're in. You want La Centerra walkable, you want Memorial Hermann Katy two miles away, you want the full infrastructure of a mature community without waiting for it to arrive. You want the best of what's already been built.

✦ Who Belongs in Fulshear

You work remotely or make the commute two to three days a week. You want a larger lot and newer construction for the same money. You like the idea of a community that still has frontier energy — where the restaurants are still opening, the trails are still being built, the story is still unfolding. You value open space and lower traffic density over proximity to established commercial corridors. Or you simply love Cross Creek Ranch and that's the end of the analysis.

✦ The Best Way to Decide

Drive both on a Saturday morning. Get an H-E-B in Cinco Ranch, walk La Centerra. Then drive out FM 1093 to Cross Creek Ranch — walk the trails, have breakfast at one of the new spots. The communities will speak for themselves. I've watched that exercise settle more "Katy or Fulshear?" debates than any spreadsheet ever has.
