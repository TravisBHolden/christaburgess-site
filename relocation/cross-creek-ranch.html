Johnson Development

A Community Built Around the Land

Most master-planned communities treat the natural landscape as a liability to be managed — fill it, grade it, plant turf, call it done. Johnson Development took a different approach at Cross Creek Ranch. The 3,200-acre community in Fulshear was designed around the existing creek corridors, native plant communities, and natural topography rather than against them. The result is a community that looks and feels different from the moment you turn off FM 1093.

Cross Creek Ranch consistently ranks among the top-selling master-planned communities in the nation — a designation that reflects not marketing but actual families choosing to live here year after year. That's the measure that matters.

✦ Cross Creek Ranch at a Glance

  • Size: ~3,200 acres
  • Developer: Johnson Development (Fulshear, TX)
  • Location: FM 1093 corridor, Fulshear, Fort Bend County
  • Schools: Katy ISD or Lamar CISD — varies by section (verify)
  • Housing: Townhomes through custom estate homes

  • 55+ section: Bonterra at Cross Creek Ranch (Del Webb)
  • Ranking: Consistently top-10 master-planned community nationally

The Amenities

The Rec at Cross Creek Ranch

The primary amenity center — The Rec — anchors the community with resort-style pools, a fitness center, event spaces, and activity programming designed for families at every stage. The amenity package isn't as water-park-large as Cinco Ranch's Beach Club, but it's thoughtfully designed and well-maintained. Multiple smaller amenity centers are distributed across the community so that no neighborhood is isolated from the activity network.

Community programming at Cross Creek Ranch has a reputation for being particularly active — seasonal events, fitness classes, social gatherings, and family programs are part of what residents mean when they describe the community as having real social infrastructure.

The Land

Trails Through Native Texas

✦ What Makes These Trails Different

Most subdivision trails are concrete paths between mowed turf corridors. Cross Creek Ranch's trail system runs through preserved native landscape corridors — creek buffers with native grasses, woodland edges with intact understory, seasonal wildflower areas maintained as pollinator habitat.

This is partly aesthetics and partly engineering: the creek corridors serve as the community's drainage system, and maintaining native vegetation in those corridors is both ecologically sound and stormwater-smart. Residents benefit from trails that actually feel like they're moving through landscape, not just moving around a development footprint.

In spring, the creek corridors carry wildflowers. In fall, migrating songbirds use the woodland corridors as stopover habitat. If you have children who are curious about the natural world, Cross Creek Ranch's trail network is one of the best educational landscapes in the Katy-Fulshear area.

Finding Your Fit

Housing Across the Spectrum

Cross Creek Ranch was designed to serve buyers across a wider range of life stages and budgets than most single-community developments. Townhome sections provide an entry point for first-time buyers or downsizers who want community amenities without single-family maintenance. Mid-range detached homes on 55–70 ft lots make up the largest share of the community. Executive home sections on wider lots occupy the established western portions of the development. Custom estate home opportunities exist in the most premium sections.

The price range runs from approximately $280K (townhomes) to $1.5M+ (estate sections). New construction is still actively available from multiple national and regional builders — allowing buyers to select plans, elevations, and finishes rather than inheriting someone else's choices.

✦ The School District Question — Read This First

Cross Creek Ranch straddles the Katy ISD / Lamar CISD boundary. Which district your children attend depends entirely on which section of the community your home is in — and the line is not drawn intuitively on the ground. Always verify the school district for the specific address at both katyisd.org and lcisd.org before going under contract. This is not a step to defer to closing day. It is a step to complete before making an offer.

The Neighborhood

The FM 1093 Dining & Retail Corridor

Fulshear's main street is FM 1093 — Westheimer Parkway extended west from the Houston suburbs into what was, until recently, open country. The commercial corridor along FM 1093 is in active growth mode: new restaurants opening, an H-E-B anchoring grocery needs, fitness studios, medical offices, and boutique retail filling in steadily. The infrastructure is present; the density is still arriving.

For Cross Creek Ranch residents, this is the lifestyle context to understand: you're in a community that already has what you need, with more arriving regularly. Buyers who find energy in that kind of growth often love it here. Buyers who want everything built and settled already should weigh Katy more heavily.

✦ Bonterra: The 55+ Chapter Within This Community

Bonterra at Cross Creek Ranch is a Del Webb active adult community located within the larger Cross Creek Ranch development. It shares the trail system and some amenities with the broader community while operating its own 10,000 sq ft clubhouse — The Club at Bonterra — with resort pools, gym, tennis, pickleball, bocce, and a full lifestyle director calendar. If you or a family member are in the 55+ buyer category, Bonterra deserves its own dedicated look. See Chapter VI for the full story.
