A Different Kind of Community

What Makes 55+ Living Different

The appeal of an active adult community isn't about subtraction — it's about addition. When your neighborhood is populated by people at the same life stage, the community builds around your actual schedule. Fitness classes at 9am. Pickleball at 10. A social calendar that doesn't revolve around school pickup. Neighbors available for a walk on a Tuesday afternoon because their week looks like yours.

The maintenance-focused lifestyle is part of it — many 55+ communities handle exterior maintenance within the HOA structure, reducing the weekend project load that comes with a traditional single-family home. But the social infrastructure is often what buyers value most after they've lived in it for a year.

The west Houston area has two 55+ communities worth knowing: Heritage Grand at Bridgeland (Katy area) and Bonterra at Cross Creek Ranch (Fulshear). They are different in character and scale. Here's the honest picture on both.

✦
Katy Area

Heritage Grand at Bridgeland

Heritage Grand is an active adult 55+ community in the Katy/Bridgeland area — gated, well-maintained, and designed for the active retirement lifestyle. The community occupies an established position within the larger Bridgeland development, giving residents access to Bridgeland's extensive trail and lake system while living in an age-restricted section with neighbors at a similar life stage.

Homes in Heritage Grand are single-story and detached, well-suited to buyers who are thinking ahead about long-term accessibility. The gated entrance provides a level of security that active adult buyers often prioritize. Location within the Katy area means proximity to Memorial Hermann Katy, Houston Methodist West, and the full retail and dining infrastructure of the Katy corridor.

✦ Heritage Grand at a Glance

  • Location: Katy/Bridgeland area
  • Age restriction: 55+
  • Gate: Yes — gated community
  • Home style: Single-story detached homes
  • Access to Bridgeland amenities: Trails, lakes, parks
  • Medical proximity: Memorial Hermann Katy, Houston Methodist West
  • Schools: N/A for residents, but grandchildren visit Katy ISD area
✦
Fulshear · Del Webb

Bonterra at Cross Creek Ranch

Bonterra is the 55+ active adult community built by Del Webb within the larger Cross Creek Ranch development in Fulshear. It is the more prominent of the two west Houston 55+ communities — larger, more nationally recognized, and built to the Del Webb standard that active adult buyers across the country have come to trust.

The community sits within Cross Creek Ranch, sharing access to the development's trail system and some amenities — but operating its own dedicated active adult campus. The Club at Bonterra is the centerpiece.

✦ The Club at Bonterra — What's Inside

  • Clubhouse: 10,000 sq ft dedicated to resident programming
  • Resort pool & spa: Outdoor resort-style pool with lap lanes and spa
  • Fitness center: Full-service gym with cardio and weight equipment
  • Tennis: Multiple courts, organized leagues and open play
  • Pickleball: Dedicated courts — one of the community's most active programs
  • Bocce: Courts with organized league play
  • Cards & billiards room: Social gaming spaces for organized club play
  • Lifestyle director: Full-time staff managing the social and activity calendar
  • Clubs & activities: 80+ clubs and organized activities

The 80+ clubs number is worth pausing on. Bonterra's lifestyle director doesn't just announce activities — they run a genuine social infrastructure. Garden clubs. Book clubs. Travel groups. Fitness classes at multiple skill levels. Creative arts programming. Charitable and volunteer organizations. For buyers moving from an active professional life into retirement, this kind of structured social calendar can be the difference between a retirement that expands and one that contracts.

✦ Bonterra at a Glance

  • Location: Within Cross Creek Ranch, Fulshear, Fort Bend County
  • Developer: Del Webb (PulteGroup)
  • Age restriction: 55+
  • Home style: Single-story detached homes, various plans
  • Access: Shared access to Cross Creek Ranch trails
  • Medical proximity: Fort Bend County medical facilities; Katy area hospitals ~25 min
  • FM 1093: Growing dining and retail corridor nearby

Before You Buy

HOA Considerations for 55+ Buyers

✦ What to Review Before Signing

Age verification requirements: Most 55+ communities require that at least one occupant be 55+ and that no permanent residents are under 18. Grandchildren visiting is fine; grandchildren living there permanently is not. Understand the exact policy in writing before you buy.

Maintenance coverage: HOA structures vary significantly. Some 55+ communities include exterior maintenance (lawn care, roof, paint) in dues; others do not. Understand what your dues cover before comparing prices — a $500/month HOA that includes exterior maintenance may be a better value than a $200/month HOA that doesn't.

Reserve fund health: Request the most recent reserve study. A well-funded reserve means the community can handle major repairs (pool resurfacing, clubhouse roof, street repairs) without special assessments. An underfunded reserve is a financial risk that doesn't show up in the list price.

Resale marketability: 55+ communities have a naturally narrower buyer pool than unrestricted communities. In strong markets this matters less; in slow markets it matters more. Understand the historical resale velocity in the specific community before you buy.
