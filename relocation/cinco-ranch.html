Origin Story

Built from Ranch Land Up

In the late 1980s, Newland Communities began transforming more than 8,000 acres of ranchland west of Houston into what would become one of the most studied master-planned communities in the country. Cinco Ranch — named for the five ranches that originally occupied the land — set a template that developers across the Sun Belt would spend the next three decades trying to replicate.

The formula: integrate amenities so thoroughly into the fabric of daily life that residents don't have to leave the community to live well. Trail systems that connect neighborhoods to pools, parks, and commercial centers. School campuses built at community scale. A retail hub — La Centerra — designed to feel like a town square, not a strip mall. Over thirty-five years, the formula has held.

✦ Cinco Ranch by the Numbers

  • Total area: 8,000+ acres
  • Developer: Newland Communities (late 1980s)
  • Subdivisions: Dozens of distinct neighborhoods across all price points
  • Schools: Katy ISD — Cinco Ranch HS, Seven Lakes HS
  • Amenity clubs: Multiple beach clubs including the Cinco Ranch Beach Club
  • Commute: 15–25 min to Energy Corridor via I-10
  • Price range: ~$320K starter homes to $1M+ luxury estates

The Amenities

The Beach Club Experience

The Cinco Ranch Beach Club is not a metaphor. It is a genuine resort-style water park attached to a master-planned community. Lazy rivers. Waterslides. Resort pools with separate lap swimming and family areas. A sandy beach area for young children. The kind of amenity that makes children enthusiastic about where they live and makes parents feel like their HOA dues are actually accomplishing something.

Multiple neighborhood-level amenity centers supplement the main Beach Club — smaller pools and parks distributed across the community so that no subdivision is far from water. The trail system ties them together: more than 30 miles of hike-and-bike trails connecting neighborhoods, parks, and community centers into a coherent network.

✦ Trail System Note

The trails aren't an amenity add-on — they're the connective tissue of the community. Families walk to school, bike to La Centerra, run to the pool. In a Texas climate where summer heat discourages being outside for six months of the year, a well-shaded trail network with water access changes the quality of life calculation significantly. Cinco Ranch's trails have mature tree canopy in the established sections. That shade matters.

The Community Living Room

La Centerra at Cinco Ranch

✦ La Centerra — More Than a Shopping Center

Most master-planned communities have retail. Cinco Ranch has La Centerra — and there's a meaningful difference. La Centerra was designed as a walkable, mixed-use town square with a central green space that hosts community events year-round. Farmers markets. Holiday celebrations. Outdoor movie nights. Live music.

The tenant mix skews toward restaurants and experiential retail rather than big-box: curated dining, a movie theater, boutiques alongside the practical anchors. Families come here on Friday nights not because there's nothing else to do — there's plenty — but because it's genuinely pleasant.

For many Cinco Ranch residents, La Centerra is the community's living room. It's where neighbors run into each other. Where kids have their first restaurant experiences. Where the abstract idea of community becomes something concrete. Not every master-planned development manages to build a place like this. Cinco Ranch did.

Katy ISD

Schools

Cinco Ranch is served by Katy ISD — consistently ranked among the top school districts in Texas and nationally. The two flagship high schools serving the community are:

Cinco Ranch High School

23440 Cinco Ranch Blvd, Katy, TX

One of the most academically decorated high schools in the Houston metro. Strong AP program, competitive UIL performance, robust fine arts and athletics. Home of the Cougars.

Seven Lakes High School

9251 S Fry Rd, Katy, TX

Serves the western Cinco Ranch area and portions of Grand Lakes. Consistently high academic ratings. Active STEM program. Strong community identity and athletics. Home of the Spartans.

✦ A Note on Boundaries

Which high school your children attend depends on the specific address — Cinco Ranch Boulevard is roughly the dividing line, but it's not absolute. Always verify the campus boundary at katyisd.org before going under contract. In a community this size, your street matters.

Community Character

Housing & Community Diversity

Cinco Ranch is not a single neighborhood — it's dozens. Starter home subdivisions sit alongside established executive communities. The price range runs from the low $300s on the eastern edges to well above $1M in premium lakefront and golf course sections. This variety means multiple entry points for buyers at different life stages.

The community is genuinely diverse — South Asian, East Asian, Latin American, and European families have established roots here over decades of development. The Katy area broadly is one of the most diverse suburban communities in the country, and Cinco Ranch reflects that character throughout. ESL programs are available across the district, and cultural celebrations are a regular feature of community life.

✦ What to Watch For When Buying

Not all parts of Cinco Ranch are created equal in terms of school zoning, HOA governance, and flood risk. The older eastern sections were built before modern stormwater management standards. The newer western sections generally have better drainage infrastructure. Always get a flood zone determination at the address level — don't assume because it's Cinco Ranch that it's out of the floodplain. And confirm the HOA — there are multiple associations with different fee structures across the community.
