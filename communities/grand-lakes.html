If you're looking for generous lots, mature landscaping, and a neighborhood that's already proven its staying power, Grand Lakes delivers. Developed in the early 2000s, Grand Lakes sits south of I-10 between the Grand Parkway and Fry Road, putting you minutes from LaCenterra, Katy Mills, and the Energy Corridor. It's the kind of neighborhood where front porches get used, the streets are tree-lined, and home values have held strong through every market cycle. What sets Grand Lakes apart from newer communities is the space. Lots here tend to run larger than what you'll find in current master-planned developments. Many homes back up to the golf course, lakes, or green belt areas — the kind of views that don't come standard anymore. --- ## QUICK FACTS | | | |---|---| | **Location** | Katy, TX 77494 | | **Established** | Early 2000s | | **Homes** | ~2,400 | | **Price Range** | ~$350K – $900K+ | | **School District** | Katy ISD | | **HOA** | Yes — Grand Lakes Community Association | | **Golf** | Willow Fork Country Club (private) | --- ## SCHOOLS Grand Lakes is served by **Katy ISD**: **Elementary:** - Griffin Elementary - Fielder Elementary **Middle:** - Beck Junior High **High:** - Seven Lakes High School — one of Katy ISD's top-rated campuses --- ## AMENITIES - **Willow Fork Country Club** — 18-hole championship golf course, pool, tennis, dining, social events - **Community Pool & Splash Pad** — Separate from the country club, open to all residents - **Parks & Green Spaces** — Walking trails, pocket parks, playgrounds - **Lakes** — Multiple stocked lakes throughout the community - **Tennis Courts** — Community courts available to residents - **Location** — Walking distance to LaCenterra at Cinco Ranch shopping and dining --- ## LIFESTYLE Grand Lakes attracts established families and professionals who want a settled, mature neighborhood without sacrificing access. The country club is a social anchor for many residents, but membership isn't required — plenty of homeowners enjoy the community without it. The neighborhood has a quieter, more established feel compared to the newer developments popping up further west. Trees are grown in. Neighbors know each other. The HOA keeps common areas well maintained. It's a community that's past the growing pains and into its stride. For golf enthusiasts, the Willow Fork course winding through the neighborhood is an obvious draw. But even non-golfers appreciate what it provides: green space, views, and a buffer that keeps the neighborhood feeling open. --- ## WHAT TO EXPECT ON THE MARKET Grand Lakes resale homes represent strong value in the Katy market. You're getting established landscaping, proven construction, and often more square footage per dollar than comparable new builds. Homes in the $400K–$600K range move quickly, especially those with golf course or lake views. Because it's a fully built-out community, inventory depends entirely on resales — there's no new construction here.
